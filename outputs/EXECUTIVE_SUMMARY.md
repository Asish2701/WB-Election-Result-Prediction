## Four Margin-Squeezing Factors

### 1. **Voter Roll Purge** (91 Lakh Deletions)
- **Impact:** 5-35% margin squeeze
- **Worst affected districts:**
  - Murshidabad (35% squeeze)
  - Maldah (30% squeeze)
  - Uttar Dinajpur (28% squeeze)
- **Key stat:** In 140+ constituencies, deleted voters exceed 2021 winning margins
- **Winner:** BJP (opposition voters previously intimidated, now deleted from rolls in TMC strongholds)

### 2. **RG Kar Medical College Case** (Women Voter Disillusionment)
- **Impact:** 2-12% margin squeeze
- **Focal point:** Panihati (AC 111) - 12% squeeze
  - BJP fielded Ratna Debnath (victim's mother), creating symbolic political issue
  - Women comprise ~49% of voters; traditional TMC support base
- **Affected areas:**
  - Urban constituencies: 5% squeeze
  - Rural areas: 2% squeeze
- **Winner:** BJP (perceived empathy with victims)

### 3. **Corruption & "Lawlessness" Narrative** (Industries Fleeing)
- **Impact:** 2-12% margin squeeze
- **Key incidents:**
  - Gherao of judicial officers in Maldah (12% squeeze)
  - Claims of investor flight and insecurity
- **Affected areas:**
  - Barddhaman industrial region: 8% squeeze
  - Hugli industrial corridor: 6% squeeze
  - Maldah: 12% squeeze
- **Winner:** BJP ("Poriborton" change narrative)

### 4. **Sandeshkhali Aftermath** (Removal of Voter Intimidation)
- **Impact:** 1-15% margin squeeze  
- **Key change:** Sheikh Shahjahan (TMC strongman) now in jail
  - Residents report feeling free to vote without fear for first time in decades
  - Opposition voters can express preferences openly
- **Geographic impact:**
  - Sandeshkhali (AC 123): 15% squeeze (epicenter)
  - North 24 Parganas riverine areas: 8% squeeze
- **Winner:** BJP (beneficiary of freed voters)

## Methodology Notes

**Squeeze Factor Calculation:**
- Individual squeeze factors summed per constituency
- Capped at 25% per seat (realism constraint)
- Applied as multiplicative reduction to TMC probability, 70% benefit to BJP

**Data Sources:**
- 2021 Assembly election margins
- 2019/2024 Lok Sabha turnout/margin trends
- Reported voter deletion statistics
- Historical district-level voting patterns

**Limitations:**
- Does not account for last-minute campaign events
- Assumes uniform swing within districts
- Cannot predict candidate-specific effects
- Probabilities are aggregate estimates

## Conclusion

The West Bengal Assembly Election 2026 remains **highly competitive**. While TMC is likely to retain power, four major margin-squeezing factors—voter roll purge, RG Kar disillusionment, corruption narrative, and Sandeshkhali's intimidation removal—combine to reduce the majority margin from **22 seats to 19 seats** and create **3-4 high-risk constituencies** where flips are plausible.

**The election is TMC's to lose, but the margin is tighter than the baseline model suggested.**

**May 4, 2026 will be determinative.**
